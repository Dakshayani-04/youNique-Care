Diet Tips for Reducing Pigmentation

Your diet plays a major role in managing pigmentation. Foods rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals help even out skin tone by reducing melanin production and preventing oxidative stress.

Vitamin C-Rich Foods

Oranges, kiwi, papaya, and lemons are loaded with vitamin C, which brightens the skin, reduces dark spots, and helps inhibit melanin production.

Zinc-Rich Foods

Pumpkin seeds, chickpeas, and whole grains are good sources of zinc, which promotes skin repair and reduces hyperpigmentation.

Carotenoid-Rich Foods

Carrots, sweet potatoes, and pumpkin are rich in beta-carotene, which protects skin from sun damage and reduces pigmentation over time.

Hydrating & Detoxifying Foods

Cucumber, coconut water, and mint help flush out toxins and keep the skin hydrated, promoting a more even tone and reducing dullness.
